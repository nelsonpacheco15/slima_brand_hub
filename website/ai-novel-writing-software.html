The problem

Why AI forgets your novel.

Most AI forgets your story after a few chapters. Paste a manuscript into raw ChatGPT or Claude and you spend more time managing context than writing. Slima is the AI writing assistant that remembers instead.

📋

Endless re-pasting

Every session starts with you copying in chapters, summaries and character notes just so the model knows what you wrote yesterday.

🧶

Losing the thread

Past the context window, the story slips. Subplots, motives and timelines drift, and the answers stop matching your book.

😶

Character amnesia

A character's eyes change colour. A dead sister reappears. The AI cannot keep your cast straight because it never read the whole thing.

Questions

Good to know.

Does Slima write my novel for me? –
No. Slima is a coach, not a generator. The AI has read your entire manuscript and answers grounded in your actual words, so it can help you get unstuck, talk through a scene, or catch a contradiction. But it does not write the book for you. You keep your voice and stay in control of every sentence.
Does the AI remember my whole book? +
Yes. Full-book memory means the coach has read every chapter and keeps the whole story in mind. It does not forget your novel after a few chapters the way raw ChatGPT or Claude does, so you never have to re-paste context or remind it who your characters are.
Is my manuscript used to train AI? +
Never. Your work is private and is never used to train models. Your manuscript stays yours.
Can I use Claude or ChatGPT with Slima? +
Yes. You can connect the AI you already use, including Claude, Cursor and ChatGPT, to your book through Slima MCP, so the assistant you prefer can read your manuscript and answer grounded in your actual story.
