The short version

Two good tools for different writers.

NovelCrafter is flexible, affordable novel-writing software with a loyal community. Its Codex story bible and bring-your-own-API-key approach let you choose your own models and keep costs low — real strengths if you like tuning your setup. Slima takes the opposite stance: it organizes your entire book in one studio, reads the whole manuscript, and checks continuity for you, so the memory work happens automatically. If you want control and model choice, NovelCrafter is a strong fit. If you want a calm coach that already knows your story, that's where this Slima vs NovelCrafter comparison points to Slima.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Is Slima a NovelCrafter alternative? –

Yes. Slima is a NovelCrafter alternative for writers who want full-book memory and continuity handled automatically instead of maintaining a manual Codex. Where NovelCrafter gives you a flexible story bible you update by hand and the freedom to plug in your own AI models, Slima organizes your whole book in one studio and gives you a coach that has already read the entire manuscript.

Do I need my own API key to use Slima? +

No. Slima works out of the box with no API key required. Unlike bring-your-own-key tools where you configure model access before you can use AI, Slima's coach and Beta Readers are ready the moment you open the studio.

Does Slima support MCP? +

Yes, natively. The Slima MCP connects Claude, Cursor, and ChatGPT directly to your book, no third-party bridge required. You can read more on the Slima MCP page.

Does Slima have a free plan? +

Yes. Slima has a free plan so you can start organizing a book and working with the coach at no cost. Pro is roughly $16 to $20 per month for writers who want the full toolkit. See pricing.
