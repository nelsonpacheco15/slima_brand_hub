Ask the coach about your book.

Last updated June 2026 · 3 min read

The coach is the part of the Writing Studio that has read your whole book. Instead of generic writing advice, it answers from inside your story, using the chapters you have actually written. Here is how to use it.

Open the coach

The coach lives in the right-hand panel of the editor. Open any project, then click the coach icon in the activity bar to bring up the chat. It loads with your current chapter already in context.

Ask a question

Type a question the way you would ask a trusted reader. The coach works best with specific, story-level questions:

  1. Ask about a character: "Is Eleanor's voice consistent between chapters three and seven?"
  2. Ask about pacing: "Where does this chapter drag?"
  3. Ask about structure: "Does the ending pay off what I set up in the first act?"
Tip. The more of your book you have written, the better the coach answers. It reads up to roughly a full novel's worth of context per question.

Point it at specific chapters

By default the coach reads the chapter you are in. To widen or narrow that, mention chapters by name or use the context picker above the message box. This is useful when you want feedback on an arc that spans several chapters.

Is my writing used to train models?

No. Each question reads your work, answers, and forgets. Your book is never used to train any model and is never shown to anyone else. For the full policy, see Privacy.
