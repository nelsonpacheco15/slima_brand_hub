# Slima — TikTok / Reels launch plan (BookTok + WritingTok)

Six short screen-recordings to launch on TikTok (and repost as Instagram Reels + YouTube Shorts). Each proves one thing by showing it. Watched on mute, so every point is on-screen text. Voice is the same as everywhere: plain, short, positive, no emojis, no colons, no negation, no "manuscript", no "flag", no leading with "AI".

## Format rules (all videos)
- **Size:** 1080×1920 vertical. **Length:** 15–30s. **Cursor:** slow and deliberate, no fast jerks.
- **First 1 second is the whole game.** Open on the hook text over the first meaningful frame. No logo intro.
- **On-screen text:** one short line at a time, high contrast, IBM Plex Serif for headers to match brand. Keep it readable at a glance.
- **Audio:** a quiet trending sound at low volume (check TikTok's trending panel the day you post). No talking needed.
- **End card (last 2–3s):** "Slima. Your whole book, in one place." + "Link in bio."
- **Record clean:** use a real-looking book/project with real chapter names, not lorem ipsum. Blur or fake any personal data.

## Video 1 — The honest read (flagship, post first)
**Why:** hits the core promise and the "is my book any good" fear. Widest appeal.
**Hook (frame 1):** Something read my entire book and marked the exact spot readers get bored.
**Shoot:**
1. Open a chapter in Slima (0–3s).
2. Click the read / beta-read action. Brief loading (3–6s).
3. The attention curve draws in. Let it finish (6–12s).
4. Slow zoom on the dip. Text overlay: "This dip is where readers drift."
5. Land on the marked drop-off point. Text: "Fix this chapter first."
**Caption:** writing a book and not sure it holds up? this reads the whole thing and shows the exact place people lose interest. that one spot is worth more than a hundred nice comments.
**Hashtags:** #writingcommunity #booktok #writingtok #amwriting #selfpublishing #writertok #novelwriting

## Video 2 — The continuity catch
**Why:** the most concrete, screenshot-able "aha". Great for the self-publisher fear of a bad review.
**Hook (frame 1):** My main character had blue eyes in chapter 2. By chapter 19 they were grey.
**Shoot:**
1. Show the chapter-2 line describing blue eyes (0–4s).
2. Cut to Slima's character detail catching the mismatch, a marker turning from calm to amber (4–10s).
3. Text overlay: "It remembers every character across the whole book."
**Caption:** the small stuff drifts in every long project. a name spelling, a day of the week, an eye colour. this catches it before a reviewer does.
**Hashtags:** #selfpublishing #booktok #writingcommunity #amwriting #indieauthor #writingtok

## Video 3 — Never lose a draft
**Why:** relatable pain, satisfying to watch. Speaks to finishers and rewriters.
**Hook (frame 1):** Every draft I have ever written of this book is still here.
**Shoot:**
1. Show the version history / timeline (0–4s).
2. Jump back three versions, then forward again (4–12s). Text: "Rewrite boldly. Everything stays."
**Caption:** write the bad draft. you can always come back. every version you have ever written is kept, so you never write scared.
**Hashtags:** #writingcommunity #amwriting #writingtok #booktok #novelwriting #writerscommunity

## Video 4 — It read the whole book
**Why:** demonstrates whole-book memory, the thing generic tools fail at. Good "vs the tools you use now".
**Hook (frame 1):** I asked it a question only someone who read my whole book could answer.
**Shoot:**
1. Type a question that needs full context, e.g. "which earlier scene sets up the ending?" (0–6s).
2. Show the answer referencing a specific early chapter (6–14s). Text: "It keeps your whole story in front of you."
**Caption:** most tools forget your book a few pages in. this one holds the whole thing, so the answer actually knows your story.
**Hashtags:** #writingcommunity #writingtok #amwriting #booktok #aitools #novelwriting

## Video 5 — Word doc to organized book
**Why:** removes the switching-cost objection. Fast, satisfying.
**Hook (frame 1):** Watch a 300-page Word file become an organized book in one drag.
**Shoot:**
1. Drag a .docx into Slima (0–4s).
2. It becomes a structured project with chapters in the sidebar (4–12s). Text: "Your whole project, in one place."
**Caption:** bring what you already wrote. one drag and it is organized by chapter, ready to work in.
**Hashtags:** #writingcommunity #amwriting #selfpublishing #writingtok #booktok #writingtips

## Video 6 — For the researchers (PhD / nonfiction)
**Why:** owns a niche with almost no competition on BookTok. Targets PhD + nonfiction.
**Hook (frame 1):** My research lived in 40 files. Now it lives in one place that remembers all of it.
**Shoot:**
1. Show scattered notes/sources being pulled into one Slima project (0–8s).
2. While writing, surface the exact source at the moment it is needed (8–16s). Text: "It remembers your sources while you write."
**Caption:** writing a thesis or a nonfiction book? the hard part was never the writing. it was finding what you already read. put the whole project in one place that remembers it, then write.
**Hashtags:** #phd #gradschool #academicwriting #phdlife #writingcommunity #nonfiction #dissertation
