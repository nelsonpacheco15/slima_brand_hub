# Helpful Threads posts (value-first, shareable)

Pure value. No product pitch in the post — the brand lives in the profile. People share these because they make *them* look useful. Notion voice: plain, short, calm. No "manuscript," no antithesis, no analogies, no em-dashes. Account: **@writewithslima**.

**H1 — scene fix (save-worthy)**
> Three questions for a scene that feels flat:
> 1. What does someone want here?
> 2. What's stopping them?
> 3. What's different by the end?
> If you can't answer all three, that's the scene to rewrite.

**H2 — revision order**
> A revision order that actually works:
> 1. Structure. Are the right scenes even here?
> 2. Scenes. Does each one move things forward?
> 3. Lines. Is it clean?
> Most people start at 3 and wonder why the book still doesn't work.

**H3 — reverse outline**
> After you finish a draft, write one line per scene: what changes in it.
> The scenes where nothing changes are the ones readers skip. That's your cut list.

**H4 — better feedback**
> "It was good" is useless feedback. Ask this instead:
> "Where did you put it down and do something else?"
> That answer is worth more than a hundred compliments.

**H5 — research system (PhD / nonfiction)**
> A research system that saves you months:
> one note per source, in your own words, with the page number.
> When it's time to write, you're arranging notes, not re-reading forty PDFs.

**H6 — character continuity**
> Keep one line per character: what they want, what they're hiding, one physical detail.
> Most continuity mistakes are just a forgotten physical detail.

**H7 — when to write**
> Writing feels hardest at night because you've spent all day making decisions.
> Writing is decisions. Do it first, before the tank is empty.

**H8 — getting started**
> If you can't start, you're trying to start well.
> Write it badly for twenty minutes and let it be bad. The rough version is the hard part to get past.

**H9 — finishing**
> Finishing a book is mostly small days. Five hundred words when you don't feel like it.
> Those are the ones that quietly add up to a finished book.

**H10 — self-publishing**
> Before you publish, read your first page out loud.
> Every place you stumble is a place a reader will too. Fix the stumbles.

**H11 — reader attention**
> Readers don't quit at the boring part. They quit a couple pages later, when they realize it isn't picking up.
> That's why you can't feel it while writing. You already know it picks up.

**H12 — capture research early**
> Organize a source the moment you find it, not later.
> "Later" is where good research goes to be forgotten.
