# Posts — Batch 01 (10 Threads + 10 Instagram)

Written in Notion's voice (see `../brand/06-social-voice-notion.md`): plain, short, calm, outcome-first. No "manuscript," no antithesis, no analogies, no em-dashes, no leading with AI. Accounts: **@writewithslima** (Threads + IG).

## THREADS (text-ready)

**T1** — The people who love your book are the worst people to ask if it's good.

**T2** — Most writers find out where their book loses people from the reviews. Too late to do anything about it.

**T3** — What quietly ends a first draft: an opening that's all backstory, a middle where nothing happens, a side character who takes over, or starting again from scratch. All fixable early.

**T4** — A year of writing. One published book. First review: "put it down around chapter 3." That was true the whole time. Nobody could see it.

**T5** — Writing a PhD is mostly not writing. It's finding the one quote, in the one file, out of forty.

**T6** — It's rarely the whole book that's the problem. It's a few chapters. Finding them is the hard part.

**T7** — The first draft is meant to be rough. That's where the book starts.

**T8** — Which chapter do you always get stuck on? Mine's right after the good opening, when things actually have to happen.

**T9** — Notion keeps your notes tidy. It just doesn't know what happens in your story. Slima reads the whole thing.

**T10** — An honest read on your book used to take weeks, or a few hundred dollars. Now it takes a few minutes.
