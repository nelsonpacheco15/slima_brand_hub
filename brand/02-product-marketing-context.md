# Product Marketing Context — Slima

*Last updated: 2026-06-22*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** The AI writing environment that remembers your entire manuscript.
**What it does:** Slima is a writing app for long-form work (novels, memoir, nonfiction, screenplays). Its AI has full-book context, it versions your drafts like code, and it runs AI "beta readers" that show where real readers would lose interest — before you publish.
**Product category:** AI writing app / writing environment for authors (not a chat assistant, not a generic doc editor).
**Product type:** SaaS (web + PWA). One account spans three studios + an MCP server.
**Business model:** Freemium. Free plan forever; Pro ≈ $20/mo (USD, global). Metered AI credits; AI Beta Reader reports consume credits.

## Target Audience
**Who:** Serious long-form writers — novelists, screenwriters, memoirists, nonfiction authors, academics/researchers. People mid-manuscript, not first-timers playing with AI.
**Primary use case:** Write and finish a long manuscript without the AI losing the plot and without losing drafts.
**Jobs to be done:**
- "Help me keep a 90k-word manuscript coherent without re-explaining it every session."
- "Let me rewrite boldly without fear of losing the version that worked."
- "Tell me where readers get bored before I send it to a publisher/agent."
**Studios:** Writing Studio · Script Studio · AI Beta Readers · Slima MCP (read/write your book from Claude/Cursor/etc.).

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Generic AI (ChatGPT/Claude in a chat box) forgets the book a few pages in — it contradicts established facts, can't hold the whole story, and every session starts from zero.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Chat assistants: no full-book memory, no structure, no versioning.
- Scrivener/Word: great organizing/typing, no manuscript-aware AI.
- Sudowrite/NovelAI: AI writing help, but weaker on whole-book memory, true version control, and reader-simulation reports.
**What it costs them:** Years of half-finished drafts; lost rewrites; books shelved because they can't tell what's wrong.
**Emotional tension:** "I've started three books and finished none." Fear the draft isn't good enough; not knowing where it loses readers.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Sudowrite, NovelAI — AI for fiction, but thinner on full-book memory + version control + beta-reader reports.
**Secondary:** ChatGPT / Claude — general AI; forgets the book, no writer workflow.
**Indirect:** Scrivener, Google Docs/Word (+Copilot) — organize/write, not manuscript-aware.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators (the three pillars):**
- **Full-book memory** — the AI has read the entire manuscript and stays consistent across it.
- **Native version control** — branch/snapshot drafts; never lose a version.
- **AI Beta Readers** — 8 distinct reader personas + a pro editor; attention curve, DNF-risk, prioritized fix-list anchored to your lines.
**Why customers choose Slima:** It's built for the *book*, not the message. It remembers, it protects your drafts, and it tells you the truth about where readers drop off.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Is my writing used to train AI?" | No — never used for training, never visible to anyone else. Used only to power your own work. |
| "How is this different from ChatGPT?" | It holds your whole manuscript, versions drafts, and simulates real readers. A chat box does none of that. |
| "Will AI write a generic book for me?" | Slima is a tool for *your* book — memory, structure, feedback — not a one-click novel generator. |

**Anti-persona:** Someone who wants AI to auto-generate a finished book with no craft involved.

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "It forgets what happened three chapters ago."
- "I keep starting over."
- "I don't know where it's dragging."
**Words to use:** manuscript, chapter, draft, version, character, plot thread, continuity, readers, finish, your book.
**Words to avoid:** "where books get finished" (banned — weak), "revolutionary", "unleash", "supercharge", hype/exclamation marks, generic "content".

## Brand Voice
**Tone:** Literary, calm, exact — like a sharp editor, not a hype marketer.
**Style:** Short, concrete, plainspoken. Specifics over adjectives. No exclamation points.
**Personality:** Writerly · precise · trustworthy · quietly confident · unflashy.
**Headline rule:** Headlines are all black (ink). Italic is reserved for the single highlighted phrase — never the whole title, never a gray/italic split.
