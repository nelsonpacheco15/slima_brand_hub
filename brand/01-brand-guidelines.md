## What Slima is

**One-liner:** The AI writing environment that remembers your entire manuscript.

It's a writing app for **long-form work** — novels, memoir, nonfiction, screenplays, research. Not a chat assistant, not a generic doc editor. The AI has full-book context, it versions drafts like code, and it runs AI "beta readers" that show where real readers would lose interest — before you publish. SaaS (web + PWA), freemium (Free forever; Pro ≈ $20/mo).

**Category:** AI writing app / writing environment for authors.

## Who it's for

Serious long-form writers, mid-manuscript (not first-timers playing with AI):
- **Indie & aspiring novelists** ★ primary — "I keep starting over." "It forgets my characters by chapter 5."
- **Self-publishing / KDP authors** ★ primary — "I don't know if it's good enough." "Reviewers DNF'd it and I never saw it coming."
- **Screenwriters & TV writers** ◦ secondary — "Continuity across episodes is a nightmare."
- **Memoir / nonfiction / academics** ◦ tertiary — "My research is scattered across 40 files."

**Always speak to all of them** — never write novelist-only copy. Use "your writing / your project / your story / your manuscript" so it lands for screenwriters and researchers too.

## The three pillars (every asset should ladder to one)

1. **Full-book memory** — the AI has read the *entire* manuscript and stays consistent across it.
2. **Native version control** — branch/snapshot drafts like code; never lose a version.
3. **AI Beta Readers** — 8 distinct reader personas + a pro editor; attention curve, DNF-risk, prioritized fix-list anchored to your actual lines.

Studios: **Writing Studio · Script Studio · AI Beta Readers · Slima MCP** (read/write your book from Claude/Cursor/etc.).

## The core tension to sell

Generic AI (ChatGPT/Claude in a chat box) forgets the book a few pages in. Scrivener/Word organize but aren't manuscript-aware. Sudowrite/NovelAI help write but are thin on whole-book memory, true version control, and reader-simulation. **Slima is built for the *book*, not the message.** The emotional truth: *"I've started three books and finished none."*

## Voice & tone

- **Warm, grounded, literary.** Plain language, short lines, one idea each. No hype, no buzzwords, no exclamation-stacking.
- **Respect the writer's craft** — Slima is a partner/studio, not a magic "write my book" button. Never imply it writes the book for them.
- **Honest, a little brave.** It tells you the truth about where readers drop off.
- Lead with the writer's pain in *their own words*, then the relief.

### Copy patterns that work
- **Question → answer:** a provoking question, then a plain-language payoff. ("Is your book any good?" → "Eight different readers and a pro editor read it and tell you the truth.")
- **Tagline:** **"Write it your way."**
- **Pillar lines:** *Write the book you keep starting over* (memory) · *Know where readers stopped, before you publish* (beta readers) · *Rewrite boldly, never lose a version* (version control) · *One home for your whole project* (organization).
- URL/handle: **app.slima.ai** · @slima_ai.

### Ad copy voice — Notion's structure + Slima's warmth (THE STANDARD)
This is how we write ads. Modeled on Notion's copywriting (studied from their live site), kept in Slima's warm, literary register. Take Notion's *structure*; keep Slima's *soul*. Never go full corporate-SaaS.

**Notion's copywriting DNA (the transferable patterns):**
1. **Short declaratives.** Three to six words. A statement, not a paragraph. *("Perfect notes, every time." / "One search for everything.")*
2. **Outcome as a finished state**, not a feature. *"Perfect continuity," not "continuity-checking tools."*
3. **Contrast pairs — "Less X. More Y."** Their signature move.
4. **The consolidation promise — "One [home] for [everything]."**
5. **"Your" / "works for you."** It serves the reader. Calm, confident, personal.
6. **Plain words, zero hype.** Sophisticated but a 12-year-old gets it. No exclamation marks.
7. **Dead-simple CTA — "Get Slima free."** (mirrors "Get Notion free")

**Slima written in this voice:**
- "The writing app that remembers your whole book."
- "One home for your whole book."
- "Perfect continuity, every time."
- "Less rereading. More writing."
- "Less starting over. More finishing."

**Two things from Notion we do NOT copy** (they break Slima's rules):
- ❌ Leading with "AI" ("The AI workspace…"). Slima never leads with AI.
- ❌ Em-dashes ("…forward—all while you sleep"). Slima uses periods/commas.

> Net: short, plain, confident declaratives and contrast pairs, outcome-as-finished-state, "one home for your whole project," CTA "Get Slima free" — written warm, not corporate, and obeying every Don't below (no AI, no em-dashes, no jargon, no chapter trope). Source studied: notion.com + notion.com/product.

### Insight-led hooks — what makes an ad CONVINCING (founder-loved, THE STANDARD)
A clean benefit line states a fact; it doesn't grab. **Open on a true thing the writer already believes (so they feel seen), then let Slima answer it.** Read every hook *as the target writer* and ask "does this hit a nerve?" If it only states a benefit, it's not done. Three reliable lenses for the hook:
- **The truth nobody says** (a little uncomfortable, instantly relatable) — strongest.
- **The dream** (what they badly want).
- **The fear** (what they dread).
Then the sub-line delivers Slima's answer in plain words. Keep the Notion structure; add the nerve.

**Umbrella word: "your whole project"** (covers novelists, screenwriters, researchers, memoirists). "book"/"story" only when the audience is clearly novelists.

**Locked example ads (this is the bar):**

*Beta Reader Studio — "An honest read, before you publish."*
- A (truth, the pick): "Your friends won't tell you the truth." / "Slima gives you an honest read, and shows you exactly where readers lose interest."
- B (dream): "Know it's good before you publish." / "Slima reads your book the way real readers would, and tells you what to fix first."
- C (fear): "Don't let readers quit halfway." / "See exactly where they lose interest, while you can still fix it."

*Writing Studio — "One home for your whole project."*

- C (confidence): "Write forward, not backward." / "Slima keeps every detail straight, so you never dig back through your own project."

CTA always: **"Get Slima free."**

### How we talk about "the AI" — DON'T
**Outward copy never leads with "AI."** Slima is a writing studio / writing partner; the intelligence is implied, not advertised. This is a hard rule (the founder has corrected it more than once).
- ❌ Never say **"your AI", "the AI", "AI that…", "AI beta readers"** in a headline or caption. ✅ Say what the writer gets: *"it remembers every word", "it has read your whole book", "eight readers give you an honest read", "a writing partner that knows your story."*
- "AI Beta Readers" / "full-book memory" are *internal/product* names — fine in docs, **not** in ad copy. In copy they're just **"beta readers"** and **"it remembers your whole book."**

### Framings we never use — DON'T
- ❌ **The "forgets by chapter 5 / by chapter N" trope** and any tech-failure framing ("does your AI forget…"). Don't sell against the machine's flaw — speak to the writer's craft and outcome. ✅ *"Keep losing the thread of your own story?"* not *"Does your AI forget your book by chapter 5?"*
- ❌ Leading copy with **chapter numbers** ("by chapter 5", "ch. 7 vs 21"). In-product screens may show light labels like "pays off later / set up early" (prefer those over "ch. 21").
- ❌ **Framing memory as the writer's failure.** Don't say "you keep losing…", "you forget…", "lose the thread." Memory is a positive capability: frame it as Slima remembering *for* them. ✅ "Perfect continuity, every time." / "It remembers your whole book." NOT "Keep losing the thread of your story?"
- ❌ "AI that writes your novel for you" / "1-click book" / get-rich-quick self-pub angles.
- ❌ Novelist-only framing. ❌ Generic-AI-assistant framing (it's not a chatbot).

### Plain language always — DON'T (founder-corrected)
**Use simple, everyday words a stranger understands instantly.** No craft jargon, no clever-but-vague phrasing. Clarity over cleverness, every time.
- **Banned words/phrases:** "slipped" (→ "notices what changed"), **"saggy" / "saggy middle"**, **"messy middle"**, **"the middle"** as a structure concept (don't describe a book by its "middle" at all), and **"thread" / "lose the thread."**
- Also avoid insider terms cold readers won't get ("the drop-off", "DNF" *except* natively on Reddit/bookish where it's common, "beat", "act two").
- ✅ Say it plainly: "where readers lose interest", "where readers stop", "the part readers skip." If your grandmother wouldn't get it in one read, rewrite it.

### Write direct, clean lines — DON'T (founder-corrected)
- ❌ **Em-dashes ( — ).** Never use them in customer-facing copy (reads as AI-written). Use a period or a comma and short sentences instead. ✅ "It reads your whole book, so you can fix it first." NOT "…your whole book — so you can fix it first."
- ❌ **Effort/time-guilt framing.** Never "You spent two years on this book…", "after all that work", "months of writing…". Don't guilt the reader about time invested.
- ❌ **Clunky meta / indirect constructions.** No "The worst reviews are the ones that say '…'", no hooks built on quoting an imagined reaction. It reads as bad writing.
- ✅ Open with a direct, simple statement or a clean question. *"Readers won't tell you where they stopped reading. Slima will."* / *"Would a stranger finish your book?"* Get to the point in plain words.

### Competitor comparisons (Notion, Scrivener, ChatGPT, Word)
Fair game, and useful (writers often organize books in Notion). Compare on **outcome, not insult**: the other tool *organizes/types*; Slima *understands your whole manuscript, keeps it consistent, and tells you the truth about it.* Frame: *"Writing your book in Notion? Notion organizes your notes. Slima understands your manuscript."* Don't disparage; state real, verifiable differences. Still no "AI" in the headline.

### Editorial line-illustration + wordplay (a house style, founder-loved)
Modeled on **The Week's** "The world at your finger*clicks*" (a pun on "fingertips") and Notion's hand-drawn ads ("Software should be beautiful"). Witty, literary, understated, print-poster calm.

**The wordplay move:** twist a familiar idiom or phrase so it gains a second, literal meaning for writing. Clever, never forced. This is Slima's version of "finger clicks."
- **"Never lose the plot."** (idiom = lose your mind/track; literal = your story's plot) ← the standout
- "Keep every character in character." (a person + staying consistent)
- "A book that reads you back." (the honest read)
- "Read between your own lines." (feedback)
- "Get the last word." (finishing / version control)
- "The plot holds." / "Word for word for word." (versions)

**The visual:** a hand-drawn **black ink line illustration** (New Yorker / editorial-cartoon style, loose confident linework) of a writerly scene, on plain cream/white paper, generous negative space, an editorial **IBM Plex Serif** headline, and **ONE accent color** used sparingly (indigo, the way The Week uses a single red bar). Charming, not corporate. Illustrations are generated (Higgsfield / nano_banana) since they are not real UI, so this style is for brand/awareness pieces, not product-proof ads.

**The refined execution (founder-loved, matches The Week's low-contrast feel):**
- Pure **white** background (`#ffffff`), everything including the illustration's own background.
- Headline: IBM Plex Serif, **weight 400** (elegant, not heavy), soft **charcoal `#3a3a40`** — NOT pure black. Big and dominant, but low-contrast. Text carries more weight than the image.
- Sub-line: serif, `ink3` (`#5a5a60`).
- Accent: an indigo underline (`box-shadow:inset 0 -13px 0 rgba(99,102,241,.9)`) on the punchline words only.
- Illustration: generate on pure white, no border/frame, then soften to **gray line-art** with `filter:grayscale(1) brightness(1.14) opacity(0.72)` (brightness clamps the paper to white; opacity greys the black lines). Size it ~70-74% width, sitting below the text.
- Footer: ✳ mark + "Slima" (serif) + `app.slima.ai` (sans, muted) on one row.
**Wordplay title bank** (idioms twisted to gain a literal writing meaning — the Slima "finger clicks" move). Keep growing this:
- *Memory / continuity:* "Never lose the plot." · "Keep every character in character." · "Total recall for your book." · "An elephant never forgets. Neither does your book." (pairs with the elephant illustration)
- *Honest read / beta readers:* "A book that reads you back." · "Read the room, before it reads you." · "Find out where readers bow out."
- *Version control / drafts:* "Get the last word." · "Rewrite history." (you can literally go back) · "Your drafts have nine lives." (pairs with the cat motif)
- *Finishing:* "Get the last word." · "Reach The End." · "From blank page to last page."
- *Worldbuilding (fantasy):* "Keep your world in one piece."
- *Research:* "Cite unseen." (sight unseen → cite unseen) · "Every source, on the same page."
- *Organization:* "Everything under one cover." · "One home for your whole project."
Non-pun but strong insight hook (fine to keep): "Your friends won't tell you the truth."

### On-brand angle: the writing journey / milestones
Slima celebrates progress (word-count milestones, streaks, daily goals — e.g. the "50,000 words — that's a novel" moment that resonates with real users). Warm, encouraging, literary, finishing-focused. A strong, safe lane — no AI talk, no tech-failure, pure writer encouragement.

## Visual system

**Logo:** the Slima asterisk/star mark (✳-like burst) + "Slima" wordmark in IBM Plex Serif. Inline SVG mark path is defined in `ads.html` (`MARK`); raster is `logo-slima.png`. On creatives the logo lives **only in the footer**, small.

**Type:**
- **IBM Plex Serif** (regular; italic for one emphasized phrase) — headlines, prose, the brand wordmark, editorial glyphs. Tracking ~ -0.02em on big heads.
- **Sans** — IBM Plex Sans (in the Remotion videos) / Inter (on web & static ads) — UI chrome, labels (uppercase, letter-spaced), stats, body.

**Color tokens:**

| token | hex | role |
|---|---|---|
| ink | `#0a0a0a` | primary text / mark |
| ink2 | `#2a2a2e` | secondary text |
| ink3 | `#5a5a60` | body / subtitles |
| ink4 | `#8a8a8e` | labels, muted, URL |

| indigo | `#6366f1` | primary accent / focus |
| green | `#4ea87a` | success / "ok" / current |
| amber | `#d97706` | warning / "look again" |
| red | `#d0463a` | error / attention-drop |

**Feel:** warm, paper-like, editorial, calm. Generous whitespace. Real product screens shown with depth (soft shadows, subtle borders, rounded 16–30px). In motion: film grain, depth-of-focus type, floating editorial glyphs, everything animated, VO-led. Static: white bg, big serif question, lots of air between title and subtitle.

**UI polish** (apply everywhere — from the make-interfaces-feel-better method): concentric radii, `text-wrap: balance` on heads / `pretty` on body, `tabular-nums` on any numbers, `scale(0.96)` on press, layered soft shadows over hard borders.
