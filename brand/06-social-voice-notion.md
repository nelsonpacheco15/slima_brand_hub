# Social & ad voice — write like Notion

The rule for all Slima social, ads, Reddit, and short-form copy: **sound like Notion.** Calm, plain, short, outcome-first. Not clever, not salesy, not "AI-sounding." We don't reinvent the wheel; we match a brand that already does this well.

## What Notion actually does (studied from notion.com)
Real headlines, verbatim: *"One search for everything." · "Perfect notes, every time." · "Keep work moving 24/7." · "You assign the tasks. Notion Agent does the work." · "Bring all your work together."* CTA: *"Get Notion free."*

The patterns:
1. **Short declaratives.** Usually under 8 words. A statement, not a pitch.
2. **Outcome as a finished state.** "Perfect notes," not "note-taking tools." Describe the result, not the feature.
3. **Clean two-part contrast** is fine: *"Less tracking. More progress."* (This is NOT the banned "you don't need X, you need Y." It's a calm outcome-contrast.)
4. **Consolidation promise.** "One home for everything." "Bring it all together."
5. **Periods for rhythm.** Commas sparingly. No em-dashes. Almost no exclamation marks.
6. **Plain words.** A 12-year-old gets it. Zero jargon, zero hype.
7. **Calm confidence.** Speaks to "you." Never preachy.
8. **Dead-simple CTA:** "Get Slima free."

## Slima in this voice — say it plainly
- ✅ "One home for your whole book." / "It remembers your whole book." / "An honest read, before you publish." / "See where readers stop." / "Get Slima free."
- Keep Slima's warmth and a little dryness, but drop the theatrics.

## Hard bans (founder-corrected — see also `01-brand-guidelines.md`)
- ❌ The word **"manuscript."** Say "your book / your story / your writing / your work / your project."
- ❌ "You don't need X, you need Y." (persuasion antithesis)
- ❌ Clever similes / analogies ("writing in Notion is like a hospital notebook…").
- ❌ Staccato three-fragment punchlines ("Organized. Pretty. No idea…").
- ❌ Leading with "AI." No em-dashes. No "chapter-N" trope.

## The test
Before posting: "Could this line sit on notion.com, and would a real person say it out loud?" If it sounds like an ad or a hook, cut it back until it's just a plain, true, short sentence.
